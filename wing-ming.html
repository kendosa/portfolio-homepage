Wing Ming

Brand Identity Design and Art Direction

Wing Ming is a flower farm and CSA rooted in the Hudson Valley. The owner grows and arranges everything herself, tending her land and her craft with equal care. As she set out to establish her presence, the goal was a brand identity that could speak to both sides of what she does: the quality and intention behind the work, and the warmth and joy of flowers themselves.

The identity balances craft with approachability, resisting the stiffness of a fine floral studio and the casualness of a roadside stand. The logomark and surrounding brand system were designed to feel considered and personal, reflecting a business built by one person with a clear point of view.

The work spans logo design, a full brand identity system, brand guidelines, print templates, production-ready assets, and art direction covering typography, color, and photography. A complete visual foundation, built to grow into from day one.

In Cantonese, 永 means permanence, the kind that outlasts seasons, trends, and time. 明 is light. Clear, warm, unmistakable. Together they are forever bright. Wing Ming came to the Hudson Valley flower market with no reputation, no referrals, and no history. Only this name and a goal to create a flower farm.

Wing Ming (永明) is an Asian, woman-owned flower farm in the Hudson Valley growing native perennials and hardy annuals in support of pollinator health and ecological reciprocity. The brief was to build a brand capable of carrying both the farm's environmental mission and the personal significance of its name. Wing Ming was Rosa's father's former business name in Macau.
